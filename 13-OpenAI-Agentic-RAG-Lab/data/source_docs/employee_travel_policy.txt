Enterprise Travel Policy

The company reimburses reasonable travel expenses when the travel is required for approved business work. Employees must use the corporate booking portal whenever possible. Travel should be booked at least fourteen days before departure unless a customer emergency or executive request requires shorter notice.

Air travel rules: Economy class is the standard option for domestic travel and for international trips under six hours. Premium economy may be used for international trips between six and eight hours when the manager approves the cost difference. Business class may be used for international flight segments over eight hours when both the department director and finance approver approve before booking.

Hotel rules: Employees should book preferred hotels from the corporate portal. The standard hotel limit is 220 USD per night in normal cities and 320 USD per night in high-cost cities. Higher hotel rates need manager approval and a short business justification.

Meals and ground transport: Daily meal reimbursement is capped at 75 USD unless the employee is hosting an approved customer meeting. Ride share, taxi, airport shuttle, and train expenses are reimbursable when they are connected to business travel. Luxury car services are not reimbursable unless approved by an executive assistant for safety or accessibility reasons.

Receipts and evidence: Receipts are required for any single expense above 25 USD. The receipt must show vendor name, date, amount, currency, and business purpose. Missing receipts require a missing receipt declaration and manager approval. All travel expenses must be submitted within ten business days after the trip ends.

Exceptions: Policy exceptions require written approval before the expense is made unless there is a safety incident, flight cancellation, medical issue, or customer escalation. Emergency exceptions must be documented in the expense report within two business days.
